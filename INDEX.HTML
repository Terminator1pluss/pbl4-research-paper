Abstract

As digital communication architectures transition toward decentralized paradigms, the security of sensitive information necessitates a multi-tiered approach addressing confidentiality, imperceptibility, and integrity. This research presents a comprehensive hybrid framework integrating Hyperchaotic Cryptography, Metaheuristic-Optimized Steganography, and Blockchain technology. We derive a three-layer protocol where 3D Lorenz maps provide high-entropy key generation, Integer Wavelet Transform (IWT) optimized via Genetic Algorithms ensures data hiding, and Blockchain provides the decentralized "proof-of-state."

Introduction

In the modern digital landscape, the concept of "privacy" has become increasingly elusive. While we rely on standard encryption protocols, these methods suffer from a fundamental flaw: they signal the presence of a secret. A block of ciphertext indicates valuable information to an eavesdropper.

This research addresses the Security Trilemma—the balance between capacity, imperceptibility, and robustness. Under the guidance of Madhu Sharma at MUJ, we have developed the CMB (Chaotic-Meta-Blockchain) architecture to bridge the trust gap in insecure networks.

The Optimization Layer

Finding the "quietest" spots in an image to hide data is an NP-hard problem. We utilize a hybrid of Genetic Algorithms (GA) for global search and Tabu Search for local refinement of the Integer Wavelet Transform (IWT) coefficients.

Genetic Algorithm

Explores the entire image landscape to find potential sub-bands for embedding without degrading structural similarity.

Tabu Search

Refines the pixel selection locally, avoiding "cycles" and ensuring a value-match between cover pixels and secret bits.

Blockchain Trust Layer

The most innovative part of this project is the decentralized witness. Once the stego-image is finalized, its SHA-3 hash is committed to a Smart Contract.
