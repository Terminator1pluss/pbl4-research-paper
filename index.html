THE CMB FRAMEWORK

A Hybrid Chaotic–Metaheuristic–Blockchain Architecture for Verifiable Transform-Domain Image Steganography.

Read the Research

Problem Statement

The rapid proliferation of unsecured digital networks has made robust information assurance critical. While cryptography ensures confidentiality, it transforms data into statistically anomalous ciphertext, acting as a beacon that invites targeted traffic analysis (violating Kerckhoffs' principle).

Steganography hides the communication's existence but is rigorously governed by the capacity-imperceptibility-robustness trade-off. Furthermore, existing steganographic frameworks possess a critical vulnerability: the implicit assumption of a benign transmission channel. They inherently lack out-of-band mechanisms to detect post-transmission carrier tampering across unsecured networks.

Literature Review

Existing models (e.g., Khalil et al., 2024) utilizing spatial-domain chaotic maps suffer from severe compounding distortions at higher payloads, restricting PSNR to ~30 dB. Alternatively, models utilizing Genetic Algorithms in transform domains (Darani et al., 2024) are susceptible to premature convergence, lacking local refinement to achieve exact value-matching.

Research Gap & Innovation

The CMB Framework directly addresses the total absence of post-transmission integrity verification in current literature. It mathematically unifies continuous-time 3D chaotic dynamical systems, evolutionary combinatorial optimization (Hybrid GA-TS), and distributed ledger technology (Ethereum PoA) for verifiable data transmission.

Experimental Setup & Constraints

  • Source Media: USC-SIPI Image Database standard testing benchmark (512x512 8-bit grayscale cover images).
  • Payload Stress Test: Evaluated at a dense capacity of 2.25 bits per pixel (bpp) to test steganalytic resistance.
  • Evaluation Metrics: Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio (PSNR), Structural Similarity (SSIM), NPCR, and UACI indices.
